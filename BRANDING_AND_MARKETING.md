**Visual Description:**
- Two hands reaching/connecting in the center, forming a bridge

**Symbolism:**
- Connection between workers and opportunities
- Bridge from kasi to career

## üé® Brand Guidelines Summary

### Typography
**Primary Font (Headings):**
- **Inter Bold** or **Poppins Bold** - Modern, friendly, highly readable
- Clean sans-serif that works on mobile screens

**Secondary Font (Body):**
- **Inter Regular** or **Open Sans** - Excellent readability at small sizes
- Optimized for mobile devices

**Accessibility:** Minimum 16px body text, high contrast ratios

---

### Voice & Tone

#### Brand Voice Characteristics:
- **Warm, not corporate** - "Welcome friend" vs "Dear User"
- **Encouraging, not patronizing** - "You've got this" vs "Let us help you"
- **Clear, not complicated** - Simple language, avoid jargon
- **Respectful, not formal** - Professional but approachable

#### Example Messages:

**DON'T:** "Proceed to commence your profile optimization"
**DO:** "Let's set up your profile"

**DON'T:** "Our platform facilitates gig-based employment opportunities"
**DO:** "Find work that fits your life"

**DON'T:** "Verify your credentials to enhance your visibility"
**DO:** "Get verified - it helps you stand out"

---

### Imagery Guidelines

#### Photography Style:
- **Real people, real settings** - Authentic South African faces and places
- **Diverse representation** - All ages, backgrounds, sectors
- **Action shots** - People working, succeeding, connecting
- **Township settings** - Don't shy away from showing real communities
- **Mobile-first framing** - Images optimized for vertical viewing

#### What to Show:
‚úÖ People smiling while working
‚úÖ Handshakes, collaboration, community
‚úÖ Township streets, homes, businesses
‚úÖ Mobile phones being used (relatable)
‚úÖ Before/after success stories

#### What to Avoid:
‚ùå Corporate stock photos (too polished)
‚ùå Poverty porn (exploitative)
‚ùå People looking defeated or desperate
‚ùå Only showing professionals (excludes target audience)

---

### Week 2: Build Anticipation

#### Goals:
- Create awareness in target communities
- Build email list for launch announcement
- Generate social media followers (target: 500+)

#### Tactics:

**1. "Coming Soon" Campaign:**
- Create landing page with email signup
- Share teaser content: "Something exciting is coming to the kasi..."
- Behind-the-scenes content (team, development)
- Countdown posts (10 days to launch)

**2. Community Engagement:**
- Join SA job-seeking Facebook groups
- Participate in township community forums
- Connect with informal sector organizations
- Reach out to local influencers (micro-influencers in townships)

**3. Content Marketing:**
- Blog post: "The State of Gig Work in South Africa"
- Blog post: "5 Ways to Stay Safe While Looking for Work"
- Infographics: Township employment statistics

---

### Week 3: Beta Launch & Testing

#### Goals:
- Get 20-30 beta testers signed up
- Generate testimonials and success stories
- Refine platform based on feedback
- Create authentic content from beta users

#### Tactics:

**1. Beta Tester Recruitment:**
- Post in community groups: "Be among the first to try MzansiGig"
- Offer incentives: Free verification, featured profiles
- Target diverse users: workers + employers

**2. Content Creation:**
- Document beta tester experiences (video)
- Share first gigs posted and completed
- Highlight success stories in real-time
- Create tutorial videos based on user questions

**3. Influencer Seeding:**
- Give early access to 5-10 micro-influencers
- Ask them to share authentic experiences
- Focus on township-based content creators

---

### Week 4: Official Launch Preparation

#### Goals:
- Finalize all launch materials
- Build launch day momentum
- Secure early media coverage
- Prepare customer support systems

#### Tactics:

**1. Media Outreach:**
- Press release: "New Platform Tackles SA Unemployment"
- Pitch to: Daily Maverick, Business Day, Tech Central, Ventureburn
- Community newspapers in townships
- Radio interviews (community radio stations)

**2. Launch Day Plan:**
- **T-3 days:** Teaser campaign intensifies
- **T-1 day:** "Tomorrow is the day!" posts
- **Launch Day:**
  - Morning announcement (7am)
  - Live Q&A on Facebook (12pm)
  - Success story shares throughout day
  - "Thank you" posts to beta testers
  - Media push

**3. Launch Content:**
- Launch announcement video (2 min)
- Platform walkthrough video (3 min)

- **Facebook Ads:**
  - Target: 18-45, South African townships, interested in jobs/gigs

  - Ad types: Video testimonials, platform benefits

  - Keywords: "find work Johannesburg", "gig jobs SA", "casual work"

**2. Community Marketing:**
- **Spaza Shop Partnerships:** Put up posters in 50+ spazas
- **Taxi Rank Promotions:** Flyers at taxi ranks (high foot traffic)
- **Community Events:** Attend township events, set up info booths
- **Street Team:** 5 brand ambassadors in key townships

**3. Referral Program:**
- "Share MzansiGig, Get Rewarded"
- Workers: Refer 3 friends, get free verification
- Employers: Refer 2 businesses, get 2 free gig posts
